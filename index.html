Tim Alamenciak

I am a translational ecologist working on engagement, open knowledge systems and communities of practice.

I believe that connecting people with nature through conservation and ecological restoration plays a crucial role in tackling the climate and biodiversity crises. I am passionate about community-engaged research, ecological art and knowledge sharing. I take a collaborative approach to academic research and teaching with an emphasis on the practical application of findings.

My work is driven by three questions: What are the best ways to support people as they engage with nature?

I believe that the answer to the climate and biodiversity crisis lies in empowering everyone - not just professionals - to care for the Earth. To achieve this goal, I research engagement and program design. My work involves a broad range of methods: surveys, interviews, geospatial analysis, strategic reviews and more.

How increase access to open ecological knowledge and data?

I am a passionate advocate of open knowledge and data. People need access to knowledge to feel empowered and care for the earth. My work involves applying semantic web methods (e.g. ontology design) to ecological knowledge and practice. Much of this work is in the software development space.

How can scientists, practitioners and community members work together better?

Vital decisions are made every day by conservation professionals. Researchers invest millions in studying our environment. Yet the two seldom talk. My work seeks to address this by building communities of practice, places where practitioners and researchers cam share insights.
