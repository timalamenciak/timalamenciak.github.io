Tim Alamenciak

I am a translational ecologist working at the intersection of community engagement, ecological restoration, and open knowledge systems. My work focuses on improving access to ecological information, strengthening collaborations between communities and scientists, and supporting deeper human–nature connections.

I use quantitative and qualitative methods—including geospatial analysis, surveys, interviews, and field research—to explore three guiding questions:

  • Engagement: How can we better support public engagement with nature?
  • Knowledge: How can ecological knowledge and data be made more open, accessible, and useful?
  • Collaboration: How can scientists, practitioners, and communities work together more effectively?
