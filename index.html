Tim Alamenciak

I am a translational ecologist working at the intersection of ecological restoration, community engagement, and open knowledge systems. My work focuses on improving access to ecological information, strengthening collaborations between communities and scientists, and supporting deeper human–nature connections.

I work across ecological science, data-driven synthesis, and community partnerships to support evidence-informed environmental decision-making. My teaching and mentoring emphasize inquiry-based learning, applied ecological problem-solving, and interdisciplinary perspectives.

I use quantitative and qualitative methods—including geospatial analysis, surveys, interviews, and field research—to explore three guiding questions:

  • Engagement: How can we better support public engagement with nature?
  • Knowledge: How can ecological knowledge and data be made more open, accessible, and useful?
  • Collaboration: How can scientists, practitioners, and communities work together more effectively?

My research spans ecological restoration, environmental knowledge synthesis, and community-driven sustainability action, with a focus on bridging scientific insights and real-world applications.
